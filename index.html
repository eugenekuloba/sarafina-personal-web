About Me
Wildlife is my love

At heart, I am an advocate for the natural world, and my mission is to contribute meaningfully to the preservation of our planet's precious biodiversity. As a research assistant, I've been fortunate to combine my love for technology and wildlife conservation in a role that constantly challenges and inspires me.

With extensive experience in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and a deep-seated fascination for spatial analysis, I wield tools like QGIS to unlock the hidden stories within geographical data. Whether it's mapping the migratory patterns of majestic elephants or assessing the impact of conservation efforts, I find immense satisfaction in using technology to aid in the protection of our planet's most magnificent creatures.

Drones have become an invaluable asset in the field of conservation, and I'm proud to say I'm not just a researcher but also a certified drone pilot. I've had the privilege of using this cutting-edge technology to monitor wildlife, gather crucial data, and aid in anti-poaching efforts. Flying drones for conservation has opened up a new dimension in my work, allowing me to view our natural world from a perspective that's both awe-inspiring and enlightening.

Conservation isn't just a job for me; it's a calling. Witnessing the splendor of elephants roaming freely across the Mara landscape or observing the delicate balance of ecosystems has fueled my determination to protect our environment. I believe that by leveraging technology, science, and the power of collaboration, we can make a profound difference in preserving our planet's ecological wonders.
